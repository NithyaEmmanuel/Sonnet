The Midnight Library

Matt Haig

This is not a book that demands attention. It waits.

The Midnight Library explores regret without dramatizing it. Each alternate life is not an escape, but a mirror.

What makes this novel worth suggesting is its restraint. It refuses to tell you how to feel.

It is best read slowly, when the day has already gone quiet.

Suggested for: reflective moods, late evenings, slow readers.
